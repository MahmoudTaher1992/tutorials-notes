# Product Manager Complete Study Guide (Ideal / Angel Method)
## Part 3: Ideal Product Manager — Execution, Launch, Metrics & Growth

---

### 7. Execution & Cross-Functional Collaboration

#### 7.1 Working with Engineering
- 7.1.1 PM's role in agile — product owner responsibilities, backlog ownership
- 7.1.2 Scrum ceremonies — planning, stand-up, review, retrospective, refinement
- 7.1.3 Kanban collaboration — flow management, WIP limits, throughput focus
- 7.1.4 Shape Up (Basecamp) — 6-week cycles, betting table, no-backlog model
- 7.1.5 Sprint planning partnership — PM sets direction, eng estimates & pushes back
- 7.1.6 Technical feasibility conversations — early engineering involvement in discovery
- 7.1.7 Definition of Done — shared completion criteria across PM, design, engineering
- 7.1.8 Avoiding micromanagement — what to build, not how to build it

#### 7.2 Working with Design
- 7.2.1 Design Thinking — empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test
- 7.2.2 PM involvement at design stage — problem framing, not pixel pushing
- 7.2.3 Wireframes vs mockups vs prototypes — fidelity levels and when to use each
- 7.2.4 Usability testing — moderated vs unmoderated, task-based sessions
- 7.2.5 Design critique — structured feedback, separating preference from principle
- 7.2.6 Accessibility (a11y) — WCAG standards, inclusive design from start
- 7.2.7 Design system alignment — consistency, PM champions the design system

#### 7.3 Working with Data/Analytics
- 7.3.1 Instrumentation ownership — PM defines what to track, eng implements
- 7.3.2 Event taxonomy — naming conventions, event schema standards
- 7.3.3 Self-serve analytics — PM fluency in Amplitude/Mixpanel/Looker
- 7.3.4 SQL basics for PMs — simple queries, filters, joins for ad-hoc analysis
- 7.3.5 Experiment setup — hypothesis, control, variant, duration, success metric
- 7.3.6 Data quality — trust but verify, QA instrumentation before launch

#### 7.4 Working with Sales & Customer Success
- 7.4.1 Feature request triage — structured intake, not raw prioritization by volume
- 7.4.2 Customer escalation handling — P0 customer issues, temporary vs permanent fix
- 7.4.3 Sales enablement — battle cards, demo scripts, objection handling
- 7.4.4 Voice of Customer (VoC) programs — regular feedback synthesis from CS/Sales
- 7.4.5 Feedback loops — CS tickets → product insights → roadmap influence
- 7.4.6 Pricing & packaging input — PM partnership with sales on deal structure

### 8. Product Launch

#### 8.1 Launch Planning
- 8.1.1 Launch tiers — T1 (major, all-hands), T2 (notable, comms plan), T3 (incremental, silent)
- 8.1.2 Launch checklist — eng readiness, docs, support training, marketing alignment
- 8.1.3 Launch brief — positioning, key messages, audiences, channels, timeline
- 8.1.4 Internal launch (soft) — dog-fooding, beta access, staged rollout
- 8.1.5 External launch — press, blog posts, social, email, in-app announcement
- 8.1.6 Launch retrospective — what worked, what didn't, process improvements

#### 8.2 Release Strategies
- 8.2.1 Feature flags — decouple deploy from release, kill switch safety net
- 8.2.2 Canary release — 1% → 10% → 50% → 100% staged rollout
- 8.2.3 Dark launch — production traffic without user visibility, load testing
- 8.2.4 Beta programs — invite-only, controlled feedback, expectation setting
- 8.2.5 Phased rollout — by geography, user segment, plan tier
- 8.2.6 Rollback plan — criteria for reverting, communication template ready

#### 8.3 Post-Launch Review
- 8.3.1 Success metrics check — did we move the needle on defined KPIs?
- 8.3.2 Qualitative feedback synthesis — support tickets, user interviews, NPS
- 8.3.3 Iteration backlog — bugs, quick wins, and follow-on improvements
- 8.3.4 Launch postmortem — what to do differently next time
- 8.3.5 Outcome vs output tracking — did the behavior change, not just the feature ship?

### 9. Metrics & Data-Driven Decisions

#### 9.1 Metric Frameworks

##### AARRR Pirate Metrics (Dave McClure)
- 9.1.1 Acquisition — how users find you (CAC, traffic sources, attribution)
- 9.1.2 Activation — first value moment (onboarding completion, aha moment)
- 9.1.3 Retention — do they come back? (D1/D7/D30 retention, churn rate)
- 9.1.4 Revenue — how you make money (LTV, ARPU, MRR, ARR, expansion revenue)
- 9.1.5 Referral — do they spread it? (NPS, viral coefficient, referral rate)

##### HEART Framework (Google)
- 9.1.6 Happiness — satisfaction, NPS, CSAT
- 9.1.7 Engagement — depth of interaction, feature adoption
- 9.1.8 Adoption — new users reaching a milestone
- 9.1.9 Retention — continued use over time
- 9.1.10 Task success — completion rate, time-on-task, error rate

#### 9.2 Key Product Metrics
- 9.2.1 DAU/MAU ratio (stickiness) — daily ÷ monthly active users
- 9.2.2 Churn rate — monthly/annual, voluntary vs involuntary
- 9.2.3 LTV (Lifetime Value) — ARPU × gross margin ÷ churn rate
- 9.2.4 CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) — total sales + marketing ÷ new customers
- 9.2.5 LTV:CAC ratio — healthy = 3:1 or above
- 9.2.6 Payback period — months to recover CAC from gross margin
- 9.2.7 NRR (Net Revenue Retention) — expansion vs contraction vs churn
- 9.2.8 Feature adoption rate — % of users who use a feature within 30 days

#### 9.3 Experimentation
- 9.3.1 A/B testing — control vs variant, one variable at a time
- 9.3.2 Multivariate testing — multiple variables simultaneously (requires more traffic)
- 9.3.3 Statistical significance — sample size calculation, p-value interpretation
- 9.3.4 Practical significance — effect size matters, not just p < 0.05
- 9.3.5 Peeking problem — stopping tests early biases results
- 9.3.6 Holdout groups — long-term control group for measuring durable impact
- 9.3.7 Experiment velocity — how many experiments per month as health metric

#### 9.4 Analytics & Funnel Analysis
- 9.4.1 Funnel analysis — step-by-step conversion, biggest drop-off identification
- 9.4.2 Cohort analysis — behavior grouped by acquisition date, plan, segment
- 9.4.3 Segmentation — behavior-based vs demographic, avoid vanity segments
- 9.4.4 Retention curves — D1/D7/D30/D90 curves, flattening = healthy product
- 9.4.5 Heatmaps & session recording — Hotjar, FullStory, Mouseflow
- 9.4.6 Avoiding vanity metrics — page views, downloads without behavioral depth

### 10. Growth

#### 10.1 Growth Frameworks
- 10.1.1 Product-Led Growth (PLG) — product as primary acquisition/expansion engine
- 10.1.2 Growth loops — viral, content, paid, product — self-reinforcing cycles
- 10.1.3 Growth vs growth hacking — sustainable systemic loops vs one-time tricks
- 10.1.4 The aha moment — moment user first feels value, design to reach it faster
- 10.1.5 Time-to-value (TTV) — reduce friction from signup to first value experience
- 10.1.6 Activation optimization — onboarding flows, empty states, guided tours

#### 10.2 Retention & Engagement
- 10.2.1 Habit loops (Hooked model) — trigger, action, variable reward, investment
- 10.2.2 Engagement ladders — from casual to power user, design the path
- 10.2.3 Win-back campaigns — churned user segments, re-activation messaging
- 10.2.4 Feature adoption drives retention — used features = higher retention
- 10.2.5 Notification strategy — push, email, in-app — value-first, not spam
- 10.2.6 Churn prediction — leading indicators, proactive intervention

#### 10.3 Monetization
- 10.3.1 Freemium — free tier as acquisition, premium as conversion, limit design
- 10.3.2 Subscription — monthly vs annual, churn math, discount for annual
- 10.3.3 Usage-based pricing (UBP) — pay for what you use, aligns cost with value
- 10.3.4 Seat-based — per-user pricing, enterprise discounts, user limit strategy
- 10.3.5 Marketplace — take rate, liquidity, supply vs demand balance
- 10.3.6 Expansion revenue — upsell, cross-sell, add-ons — grow within existing accounts
