# Software Company Roles Study Guide - Part 2: Product Roles

## 2.0 Product Roles

### 2.1 Product Manager (PM)
#### 2.1.1 Core Responsibilities
- 2.1.1.1 Define the "what" and "why" — not the "how" — outcome over output
  - 2.1.1.1.1 Problem discovery — user research + data — identify real pain points
  - 2.1.1.1.2 Opportunity sizing — market + user segment + revenue potential
- 2.1.1.2 Product strategy — vision → strategy → roadmap → backlog — cascade
  - 2.1.1.2.1 Vision — long-term aspirational state — 3–5 year horizon
  - 2.1.1.2.2 Strategy — bets + priorities — how vision is achieved — annual
  - 2.1.1.2.3 Roadmap — themes + milestones — not feature list — communicates intent
- 2.1.1.3 Backlog ownership — prioritize ruthlessly — say no more than yes
  - 2.1.1.3.1 Prioritization frameworks — RICE / ICE / MoSCoW / opportunity scoring
  - 2.1.1.3.2 RICE — Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort — quantified priority score

#### 2.1.2 PM Stakeholder Management
- 2.1.2.1 Triangulate between customers / business / engineering
  - 2.1.2.1.1 Customer — what they ask for vs. what they need — Jobs to be Done
  - 2.1.2.1.2 Business — revenue, retention, strategic fit — OKR alignment
  - 2.1.2.1.3 Engineering — feasibility, tech debt, velocity — build trust with team
- 2.1.2.2 Executive communication — concise — so what framing — decision-focused
  - 2.1.2.2.1 Product review — metrics + learnings + next bets — recurring cadence
  - 2.1.2.2.2 Escalation — know when to escalate vs. resolve at team level

#### 2.1.3 PM Discovery & Delivery
- 2.1.3.1 Dual-track agile — discovery (learn) + delivery (build) — run in parallel
  - 2.1.3.1.1 Discovery — prototype + user test — validate before building
  - 2.1.3.1.2 Delivery — sprint-based — shippable increment — no-surprise launches
- 2.1.3.2 User story format — As a [user], I want [goal], so that [outcome]
  - 2.1.3.2.1 Acceptance criteria — testable — agreed before sprint starts
  - 2.1.3.2.2 Definition of Ready — story refined enough to begin — groomed
- 2.1.3.3 Metrics & success criteria — define before building — measure after launch
  - 2.1.3.3.1 North Star Metric — single focus — reflects customer value delivered
  - 2.1.3.3.2 Counter metrics — guard rails — ensure NS metric gain is real not gamed
  - 2.1.3.3.3 Leading vs. lagging indicators — predict outcome — confirm result

#### 2.1.4 PM Career Levels
- 2.1.4.1 Associate PM (APM) — feature-level ownership — mentored — execution focus
- 2.1.4.2 PM — full product area ownership — discovery + delivery — independent
- 2.1.4.3 Senior PM — multi-area — mentors junior PMs — strategic input
- 2.1.4.4 Principal / Group PM — multiple teams — org-wide strategy — PM team lead
- 2.1.4.5 Director of Product — department — hiring + culture + roadmap alignment
- 2.1.4.6 VP / CPO — company product vision — board-level reporting — P&L ownership

### 2.2 Product Owner (PO)
#### 2.2.1 PO vs. PM Distinction
- 2.2.1.1 PO — Scrum-specific role — backlog authority — sprint ceremonies
  - 2.2.1.1.1 In many companies PM + PO = same person — especially smaller orgs
  - 2.2.1.1.2 In large enterprises — PM sets strategy — PO manages team execution
- 2.2.1.2 PO responsibilities within Scrum
  - 2.2.1.2.1 Backlog refinement — break epics → stories → tasks — sprint-ready
  - 2.2.1.2.2 Sprint planning — commit with team — shared understanding of stories
  - 2.2.1.2.3 Sprint review — accept/reject work — based on acceptance criteria
  - 2.2.1.2.4 Single source of truth for priorities — one voice to the team — no split authority

### 2.3 Technical Program Manager (TPM)
#### 2.3.1 TPM Core Responsibilities
- 2.3.1.1 Drive large cross-team technical programs — coordination over code
  - 2.3.1.1.1 Program — collection of related projects — shared goal — 6–18 month scope
  - 2.3.1.1.2 Dependency mapping — identify cross-team blockers — resolve proactively
- 2.3.1.2 Risk management — identify / assess / mitigate — program-level risks
  - 2.3.1.2.1 Risk register — likelihood × impact — owner per risk — tracked weekly
  - 2.3.1.2.2 Mitigation vs. contingency — prevent risk vs. plan for if it occurs
- 2.3.1.3 Stakeholder alignment — keep executives + teams + partners in sync
  - 2.3.1.3.1 Weekly status report — RAG status (Red/Amber/Green) — decisions needed
  - 2.3.1.3.2 Steering committee — executive sponsors — escalation path — monthly

#### 2.3.2 TPM vs. EM vs. PM
- 2.3.2.1 TPM — cross-team coordination — no direct reports — influence over authority
- 2.3.2.2 EM — people management — team delivery — owns one team's engineering
- 2.3.2.3 PM — what to build — user + business focus — not execution coordination
  - 2.3.2.3.1 Collaboration model — PM owns why/what — TPM owns when/dependencies — EM owns how/who
