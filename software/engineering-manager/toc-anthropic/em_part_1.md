# Engineering Manager Complete Study Guide (Ideal / Angel Method)
## Part 1: Ideal Engineering Manager — Foundation & People Management

---

### 1. The Role & Mindset

#### 1.1 Defining the Engineering Manager
- 1.1.1 The core mandate — maximize team output, not personal output
- 1.1.2 Force multiplier vs individual contributor — leverage through others
- 1.1.3 Servant leadership — removing blockers, clearing the path
- 1.1.4 The dual axis — people leadership + technical credibility
- 1.1.5 Manager vs leader — authority granted vs trust earned
- 1.1.6 The EM's primary product — a high-performing, growing team

#### 1.2 Role Differentiation
- 1.2.1 EM vs Tech Lead — people/org ownership vs technical direction
- 1.2.2 EM vs Product Manager — how to build vs what/why to build
- 1.2.3 EM vs Project Manager — holistic team ownership vs execution coordination
- 1.2.4 EM vs Staff Engineer — management ladder vs IC ladder, collaboration model
- 1.2.5 Tech Lead Manager (TLM) — player-coach hybrid, when it works and when it doesn't
- 1.2.6 Span of control norms — typical 5-8 direct reports, variance by company stage

#### 1.3 The IC → EM Transition
- 1.3.1 Identity shift — engineer identity vs manager identity
- 1.3.2 First 30/60/90 days — listen first, change second
- 1.3.3 Letting go of the code — when to contribute vs when to delegate
- 1.3.4 Building trust as a new manager — consistency, transparency, follow-through
- 1.3.5 Common first-time pitfalls — micromanaging, avoiding hard conversations, over-coding
- 1.3.6 Time management reboot — calendar audit, maker vs manager schedule (Paul Graham)

---

### 2. Hiring & Team Building

#### 2.1 Recruiting Strategy
- 2.1.1 Headcount planning — justifying req with business impact, capacity forecasting
- 2.1.2 Job description craft — outcomes-focused, inclusive language, avoiding laundry lists
- 2.1.3 Sourcing channels — referrals, LinkedIn, conferences, open source, bootcamps
- 2.1.4 Talent pipeline — always-on recruiting vs reactive hiring, warm candidate pool
- 2.1.5 Employer branding — tech blog, talks, open source as recruiting leverage
- 2.1.6 Recruiter partnership — working effectively with internal/external recruiters

#### 2.2 Interview Process Design
- 2.2.1 Structured interviews — rubric-based scoring, standardized questions per role
- 2.2.2 Interview loop design — phone screen, technical, system design, behavioral, bar raiser
- 2.2.3 Technical assessment options — take-home vs live coding vs portfolio review tradeoffs
- 2.2.4 Bias mitigation — blind resume review, diverse panels, structured debrief process
- 2.2.5 Candidate experience — communication, feedback, decision speed
- 2.2.6 Closing candidates — selling the role, team, mission, growth opportunity
- 2.2.7 Reference checks — structured questions, red/green flag patterns

#### 2.3 Onboarding
- 2.3.1 Day 1 experience — environment setup, introductions, team norms doc
- 2.3.2 30-day ramp plan — starter tasks, shadowing, first ship
- 2.3.3 Buddy/mentor pairing — social integration, cultural guidance
- 2.3.4 Feedback loops — 2-week, 30-day, 60-day check-ins
- 2.3.5 Early win design — structured first task for confidence and belonging
- 2.3.6 Onboarding metrics — time-to-first-commit, time-to-full-productivity

#### 2.4 Team Structure Design
- 2.4.1 Conway's Law — org structure mirrors software architecture
- 2.4.2 Team topologies — stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated-subsystem
- 2.4.3 Skill balance — seniority mix, T-shaped vs I-shaped, generalist vs specialist
- 2.4.4 Team size — two-pizza rule, Dunbar's layers, cognitive load
- 2.4.5 Reorganization triggers — growth, product pivot, technical boundary change
- 2.4.6 Distributed/remote teams — async norms, timezone-aware scheduling, documentation culture

### 3. People Development & Performance

#### 3.1 1:1 Meetings
- 3.1.1 Purpose — relationship building, not status reporting
- 3.1.2 Frequency and length — weekly 30-min default, flex based on need
- 3.1.3 Agenda ownership — employee-led, manager prepares 20% follow-up items
- 3.1.4 Question frameworks — GROW model, career ladder check-ins, blockers
- 3.1.5 Documentation — shared notes, action item tracking, pattern recognition over time
- 3.1.6 Skip-level 1:1s — sensing team health without direct report filter

#### 3.2 Career Development
- 3.2.1 Career ladders — levels, competency matrices, expectations per level
- 3.2.2 Individual Development Plans (IDPs) — 6-12 month goals, concrete milestones
- 3.2.3 Growth opportunities — stretch assignments, leading projects, conference talks
- 3.2.4 Mentoring vs coaching vs sponsoring — give advice vs ask questions vs advocate
- 3.2.5 Identifying future leaders — initiative, leverage, communication, ownership
- 3.2.6 IC vs management track — supporting the choice, no "promotion to manager" by default

#### 3.3 Performance Management
- 3.3.1 Continuous feedback — real-time, specific, behavior-focused (not trait-focused)
- 3.3.2 Performance reviews — calibration, ratings, written evaluations
- 3.3.3 Calibration sessions — cross-manager alignment, avoiding recency/halo bias
- 3.3.4 Managing high performers — retention, stretch goals, visibility, compensation
- 3.3.5 Underperformance early signs — missed expectations, attitude shifts, low energy
- 3.3.6 Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs) — clear goals, timeline, support, documentation
- 3.3.7 Letting someone go — decision criteria, legal compliance, compassionate execution

#### 3.4 Feedback & Communication
- 3.4.1 Radical Candor framework — care personally, challenge directly
- 3.4.2 SBI model — Situation, Behavior, Impact for concrete feedback
- 3.4.3 Positive feedback — recognition, public praise, specificity
- 3.4.4 Receiving feedback — modeling openness, avoiding defensiveness
- 3.4.5 Upward feedback — encouraging reports to give feedback to the manager
- 3.4.6 Written communication — Slack/email tone, async decision-making, meeting notes

#### 3.5 Delegation & Empowerment
- 3.5.1 What to delegate — tasks below your highest leverage, routine decisions
- 3.5.2 Delegation levels — tell, sell, consult, agree, advise, ask, delegate
- 3.5.3 Avoiding micromanagement — define the outcome, not the method
- 3.5.4 Psychological safety — Lencioni's trust base, Amy Edmondson's research
- 3.5.5 Autonomy with accountability — clear ownership + clear success criteria
- 3.5.6 Conflict resolution — mediate early, separate positions from interests

#### 3.6 Team Health & Retention
- 3.6.1 Flight risk signals — disengagement, external interview prep, compensation dissatisfaction
- 3.6.2 Retention levers — growth, autonomy, compensation, belonging, mission alignment
- 3.6.3 Stay interviews — proactive conversations, "what would make you leave?"
- 3.6.4 Exit interviews — learning from departures, systemic patterns
- 3.6.5 Engagement surveys — action planning, closing the feedback loop
- 3.6.6 Burnout prevention — sustainable pace, workload monitoring, PTO culture
