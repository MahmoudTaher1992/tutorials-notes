# Engineering Manager Complete Study Guide (Ideal / Angel Method)
## Part 3: Ideal Engineering Manager — Culture, Strategy & Crisis

---

### 7. Engineering Culture

#### 7.1 Defining & Shaping Culture
- 7.1.1 Culture as behavior — what the team consistently does, not what's on the wall
- 7.1.2 Team values — co-created, few, actionable, measurable in behavior
- 7.1.3 Norms documentation — team working agreements, decision-making frameworks
- 7.1.4 Rituals — demo days, hackathons, learning lunches, team retrospectives
- 7.1.5 The EM as culture carrier — modeling the behaviors you expect
- 7.1.6 Subculture within company culture — team identity aligned with org values

#### 7.2 Psychological Safety
- 7.2.1 Amy Edmondson's definition — safe to take risks, voice concerns, make mistakes
- 7.2.2 Building safety — respond well to failure, ask questions vs criticize
- 7.2.3 Blameless culture — separating person from problem in incidents and postmortems
- 7.2.4 Dissent channels — how to disagree and commit, escalation without fear
- 7.2.5 Meeting participation — ensure all voices heard, interruption norms
- 7.2.6 Measuring safety — survey questions, NPS variants, anonymous feedback tools

#### 7.3 Learning Culture
- 7.3.1 Knowledge sharing cadence — weekly tech talks, rotation of presenters
- 7.3.2 Book clubs & reading groups — engineering books, leadership literature
- 7.3.3 Failure retrospectives — learning from bugs, outages, project post-mortems
- 7.3.4 External learning budget — conferences, courses, certifications, books
- 7.3.5 Internal documentation — wikis, decision logs, architecture diagrams, ADRs
- 7.3.6 Innovation time — 20% time, hackathons, research spikes

#### 7.4 Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
- 7.4.1 Bias in hiring — structured interviews, diverse panels, job description audits
- 7.4.2 Inclusive meeting facilitation — round-robins, async options, written contributions
- 7.4.3 Equitable growth opportunities — visible projects distributed fairly across team
- 7.4.4 Pay equity — periodic audits, transparent compensation bands
- 7.4.5 Allyship in practice — amplifying underrepresented voices, intervening in bias
- 7.4.6 ERG (Employee Resource Group) engagement — supporting, not tokenizing

### 8. Crisis & Incident Management

#### 8.1 Incident Response
- 8.1.1 Incident classification — P0 (all-hands), P1 (urgent), P2 (same-day), P3 (backlog)
- 8.1.2 EM's war room role — communication hub, not technical debugger
- 8.1.3 Stakeholder communication during incidents — status page, Slack updates, cadence
- 8.1.4 Escalation tree — who to page, when, internal vs external escalation
- 8.1.5 War room facilitation — clear scribe, commander, subject matter experts
- 8.1.6 Customer communication — transparency, timeline, compensation if needed

#### 8.2 Post-Incident Review
- 8.2.1 Blameless postmortem format — timeline, contributing factors, action items
- 8.2.2 Root cause analysis — 5 Whys, fishbone diagram, not stopping at first cause
- 8.2.3 Action item ownership — named owner, deadline, linked to sprint/backlog
- 8.2.4 Systemic patterns — recurring incidents signal deeper structural issues
- 8.2.5 Sharing postmortems — internal transparency, cross-team learning
- 8.2.6 Closing the loop — verifying action items completed, follow-up review

#### 8.3 People Crises
- 8.3.1 Team conflict escalation — when mediation fails, decisive action required
- 8.3.2 Sudden attrition — single point of failure, knowledge transfer, workload re-balancing
- 8.3.3 Layoffs — communication, compassion, survivor syndrome management
- 8.3.4 Harassment or misconduct — HR partnership, documentation, protection of reporter
- 8.3.5 Mental health crisis — resources, accommodation, confidentiality
- 8.3.6 Manager burnout — recognizing your own limits, peer support, delegation up

#### 8.4 Leading Through Change
- 8.4.1 Reorganization — team identity preservation, communication cadence
- 8.4.2 Executive leadership change — adapting to new direction, protecting team stability
- 8.4.3 Product pivot — re-scoping team mission, re-skilling or re-staffing
- 8.4.4 Technology adoption — change management, training, early adopter program
- 8.4.5 Process change — buy-in before rollout, feedback channels, rollback option
- 8.4.6 Managing resistance — listening first, separating concerns from complaints

### 9. Business Acumen & Financial Management

#### 9.1 Budget & Resource Management
- 9.1.1 Headcount planning — FTE vs contractor tradeoffs, hiring lead times
- 9.1.2 Cloud cost ownership — FinOps mindset, cost attribution per team
- 9.1.3 Tooling budget — licensing, developer productivity ROI, consolidation
- 9.1.4 Business case writing — problem → solution → cost → benefit → risk
- 9.1.5 ROI framing — speed, quality, risk reduction as business value
- 9.1.6 Budget cycles — fiscal year planning, mid-year adjustments, underspend risk

#### 9.2 OKRs & Goal Setting
- 9.2.1 OKR mechanics — Objectives (qualitative), Key Results (quantitative, time-bound)
- 9.2.2 Team OKRs — cascading from company, bottoms-up contribution
- 9.2.3 OKR anti-patterns — too many, too easy, KR as task list (not outcomes)
- 9.2.4 OKR cadence — quarterly review, monthly check-in, weekly key result update
- 9.2.5 Leading vs lagging indicators — leading (behavior) vs lagging (outcome) metrics
- 9.2.6 Individual OKRs vs team OKRs — team accountability vs personal accountability

#### 9.3 Strategic Thinking
- 9.3.1 Thinking in systems — feedback loops, unintended consequences, leverage points
- 9.3.2 Prioritization frameworks — RICE (Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort), ICE, Kano
- 9.3.3 Second-order thinking — what happens after the obvious consequence?
- 9.3.4 Technical strategy documents — one-pager, six-pager (Amazon format), narrative style
- 9.3.5 Market/competitor awareness — how technology trends affect team direction
- 9.3.6 Platform thinking — internal products as leverage, build once use many

### 10. Manager of Managers (Senior EM / Director)

#### 10.1 Managing Managers
- 10.1.1 Different leverage — 1:1 with leads, not with individual ICs
- 10.1.2 Coaching managers — help them solve problems vs solving for them
- 10.1.3 Calibrating across teams — consistent standards, fair comparisons
- 10.1.4 Org design at scale — team topology, reporting structure, span of control
- 10.1.5 Identifying future managers — signals: empathy, leverage, communication, courage
- 10.1.6 Manager onboarding — structured ramp for new managers on your team

#### 10.2 Organizational Design
- 10.2.1 Centralized vs decentralized — platform teams vs embedded specialists
- 10.2.2 Team interdependency reduction — domain ownership, clear API contracts
- 10.2.3 Scaling communication — decision escalation rules, working groups, guilds
- 10.2.4 Org chart vs interaction map — formal structure vs actual collaboration patterns
- 10.2.5 Succession planning — bus factor, key person risk, leadership pipeline
- 10.2.6 Director/VP scope — portfolio thinking, multi-team delivery, executive presence
