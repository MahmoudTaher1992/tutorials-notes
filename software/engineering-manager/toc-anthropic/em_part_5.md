# Engineering Manager Complete Study Guide (Ideal / Angel Method)
## Part 5: EM Craft — Self-Development, Tools & Frameworks Reference

---

### 11. EM Self-Management & Personal Growth

#### 11.1 Managing Yourself
- 11.1.1 Time audit — tracking where time goes vs where it should go (calendar analysis)
- 11.1.2 Maker vs manager schedule — protecting deep work blocks for strategy/writing
- 11.1.3 Energy management — peak hours for hard decisions, async for low-energy work
- 11.1.4 Focus vs availability paradox — being accessible without losing strategic time
- 11.1.5 Weekly review habit — reflection, preparation, re-prioritization
- 11.1.6 Personal OKRs — goals for yourself as a manager, not just your team

#### 11.2 Developing Your EM Brand
- 11.2.1 Writing publicly — engineering blog, LinkedIn, internal documentation
- 11.2.2 Conference speaking — positioning as domain expert, team recruiting signal
- 11.2.3 Open source contribution — team visibility, technical community presence
- 11.2.4 Internal influence — leading working groups, cross-team initiatives
- 11.2.5 Building peer network — EM communities, management cohorts, Slack groups
- 11.2.6 Asking for feedback — 360 feedback process, explicit ask from reports/peers

#### 11.3 Avoiding Burnout
- 11.3.1 Emotional labor recognition — 1:1s, performance reviews, hard conversations drain energy
- 11.3.2 Boundaries with reports — availability expectations, after-hours norms
- 11.3.3 Manager loneliness — isolation of role, peer support, external communities
- 11.3.4 Your own manager relationship — psychological safety upward, asking for help
- 11.3.5 Recovery practices — vacation, deliberate disconnection, physical health
- 11.3.6 Recognizing warning signs — cynicism, exhaustion, reduced effectiveness

#### 11.4 Continuous Learning
- 11.4.1 Essential reading — *The Manager's Path* (Fournier), *An Elegant Puzzle* (Larson), *High Output Management* (Grove)
- 11.4.2 Leadership books — *Radical Candor*, *Turn the Ship Around*, *Dare to Lead*
- 11.4.3 Business acumen reading — *Good Strategy Bad Strategy*, *The Innovator's Dilemma*
- 11.4.4 Staying technical — reading RFCs, system design resources, adjacent tech exposure
- 11.4.5 Coaching & therapy — executive coaching, therapy for processing leadership stress
- 11.4.6 Management cohorts — peer learning groups, EM roundtables, mentorship programs

### 12. Frameworks & Mental Models Reference

#### 12.1 People & Feedback Frameworks
- 12.1.1 Radical Candor — 2×2: care personally / challenge directly
- 12.1.2 SBI Model — Situation, Behavior, Impact for feedback delivery
- 12.1.3 GROW Model — Goal, Reality, Options, Will for coaching conversations
- 12.1.4 Situational Leadership (Hersey-Blanchard) — directing, coaching, supporting, delegating
- 12.1.5 Lencioni's 5 Dysfunctions — trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, results
- 12.1.6 RACI Matrix — Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed for ownership clarity

#### 12.2 Prioritization & Decision Frameworks
- 12.2.1 RICE — Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort for feature prioritization
- 12.2.2 ICE — Impact × Confidence × Ease (simpler variant)
- 12.2.3 MoSCoW — Must, Should, Could, Won't for scope negotiation
- 12.2.4 Eisenhower Matrix — urgent/important 2×2 for personal time management
- 12.2.5 Opportunity Solution Tree (Teresa Torres) — connecting goals to opportunities to solutions
- 12.2.6 Disagree and Commit — mechanism for moving forward after losing a decision

#### 12.3 Team & Org Design Frameworks
- 12.3.1 Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais) — 4 team types, 3 interaction modes
- 12.3.2 Conway's Law — org structure mirrors system architecture
- 12.3.3 Dunbar's Number — 5 (trust), 15 (strong ties), 50 (working group), 150 (community)
- 12.3.4 DORA Metrics — deployment frequency, lead time, MTTR, change failure rate
- 12.3.5 SPACE Framework — Satisfaction, Performance, Activity, Communication, Efficiency
- 12.3.6 Developer Experience (DX) — measuring and improving developer productivity holistically

#### 12.4 Strategy & Communication Frameworks
- 12.4.1 Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto) — conclusion first, then supporting arguments
- 12.4.2 BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front for executive communication
- 12.4.3 Good Strategy / Bad Strategy (Rumelt) — kernel: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions
- 12.4.4 Amazon 6-Pager — narrative-style strategy document, no bullet slides
- 12.4.5 OODA Loop — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act for fast decision cycles
- 12.4.6 Systems Thinking — stocks, flows, feedback loops, delays, leverage points

#### 12.5 Career Ladders & Level Definitions
- 12.5.1 Standard EM levels — EM → Sr EM → Director → Sr Director → VP → SVP → CTO
- 12.5.2 Scope at each level — team (EM), org (Director), business unit (VP), company (CTO)
- 12.5.3 Artifacts of seniority — decision autonomy, stakeholder tier, planning horizon
- 12.5.4 Dual-track systems — IC ladder (Staff, Principal, Distinguished) runs parallel
- 12.5.5 Promotion calibration — consistent bar across managers, evidence-based
- 12.5.6 Level inflation risks — title dilution, expectations mismatch on hiring

### 13. Tooling & Systems for EMs

#### 13.1 Project & Work Tracking
- 13.1.1 Jira — enterprise standard, powerful but complex, board/backlog/roadmap views
- 13.1.2 Linear — fast, keyboard-driven, git integration, cycles (sprints)
- 13.1.3 Shortcut (Clubhouse) — mid-market alternative, stories/epics/milestones
- 13.1.4 Notion — flexible, wiki + database + roadmap, less structured
- 13.1.5 GitHub Projects — close to code, good for engineering-heavy teams
- 13.1.6 Asana / Monday — cross-functional PM-oriented tools

#### 13.2 1:1 & People Management Tools
- 13.2.1 Lattice — performance reviews, OKRs, 1:1 templates, engagement surveys
- 13.2.2 Leapsome — learning, performance, engagement, OKRs integrated
- 13.2.3 Culture Amp — engagement surveys, action planning, people analytics
- 13.2.4 15Five — weekly check-ins, OKRs, manager effectiveness surveys
- 13.2.5 Notion / Confluence — 1:1 templates, career development docs, team wiki

#### 13.3 Communication & Async Tools
- 13.3.1 Slack — real-time messaging, channel structure reflects org structure
- 13.3.2 Loom — async video for announcements, code walkthroughs, design reviews
- 13.3.3 Notion AI / Confluence — documentation, RFCs, runbooks
- 13.3.4 Miro / FigJam — async whiteboarding, architecture diagrams, retrospectives
- 13.3.5 Donut — Slack integration for random coffee pairings, remote culture building

#### 13.4 Engineering Metrics & Observability
- 13.4.1 LinearB / Swarmia — DORA metrics, PR cycle time, review depth analytics
- 13.4.2 Faros AI — engineering metrics platform, multi-source data aggregation
- 13.4.3 Datadog / Grafana — system observability (latency, error rate, uptime)
- 13.4.4 PagerDuty / OpsGenie — on-call management, escalation policies, incident tracking
- 13.4.5 Backstage — developer portal, service catalog, technical documentation hub
